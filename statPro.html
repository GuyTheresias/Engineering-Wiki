Statistical Profile of Electrical Engineering

The chart below indicates the population of electrical engineers in comparison to other related fields from 2014-2021.

Fig. 1 Electrical engineering population in relation to related fields. (2014-2021)



           One thing to note is that the numbers of these engineers has increased at a very slow rate, almost stagnant until 2018 when the numbers began to rise.

This chart displays the where electrical engineers fall when ranked by average income from the year 2021.

Fig. 2 Ranking of electrical and electronic engineers by salary (2021)



Electrical engineers do quite well for themselves, placing in 30th for highest average wage nationwide.

The color-coded maps below highlight average wage and workforce size for electrical engineers from the year 2021.

Fig. 3 Average wage of electrical engineers in 2021. Dark colors represent higher wages while light represents lower.



Fig. 4 Workforce population of Electrical engineers by state in 2021, dark colors represent high population while lighter colors represent lower.



           Data from the charts indicate that the coasts or other urbanized areas are where electrical engineers are paid the most, in higher demand, and are present in higher numbers.


The graphic below illustrates the most popular fields for electrical engineers

Fig. 5 Electrical percentages of electrical engineers employed within different job sectors.



From the graphic we see that that if we are to rank job sectors by percentage of electrical engineers employed within then:
  • Manufacturing with more than 40% of all electrical engineers employed within.
  • Architectural and engineering research and design with more than 20%.
  • Power distribution with more than 8%
  • Telecommunications with more than 7%
  • Federal government jobs with more than 4%

The chart below shows the education level achieved by electrical engineers.

Fig. 6 Education level of male and female Electrical engineers.



           From what we can see, this chart indicates that most electrical engineers have 4-6 years of college education as they represent the bulk of the profession. Only a very small percentage of engineers have no college education to speak of.
           What can also be drawn from this graph is that men vastly outnumber women in this field of work (10% women, 90% men roughly).
