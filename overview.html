Overview of Electrical Engineering

Introduction

            Electrical engineering was said to have emerged as a discipline in 1864, when James Clerk Maxwell, a Scottish physician summarized the basic laws of electricity in mathematical form and showed that radiation of electromagnetic energy travels through space at the speed of light. Maxwell then began to theorize ways to artificially recreate the discovered electromagnetic energies. Eventually a German physician named Heinrich Hertz in 1887 successfully created this phenomenon via radio waves.

            These scientific studies, combined with the industrial innovations of the telephone by Alexander Grahm Bell and Thomas Edison’s innovations such as the lightbulb and power plant combined to form the intellectual and practical groundwork for the electrical engineer. Knowledge on electrical theories and how to practically apply them for industrial use would become increasingly useful as nations built and expanded sophisticated power grids, and companies began to produce electronic appliances.

Related Fields

  • Test Engineer: responsible for conducting tests on electrical systems. When they identify problems during the tests, they consult their clients and offer guidance on implementing a solution. The average salary for a test engineer is $82,519 yearly.
  • Design Engineer: uses computer aided design to develop new systems and products. As a result, they work in close proximity to their clients to make sure their product meets the specifications required. The average salary for a design engineer is $86,519 yearly.
  • Communications Engineer: responsible for designing and developing communications systems such as satellites, radio towers, and fiber optic cables. They also assist with the installation of said communications equipment as well as assure to its quality. The average salary of a communications engineer is $99,134 yearly.
  • Aeronautical engineer: responsible for constructing systems for aircraft or satellites. They may design mechanical, communications, or electrical systems. It is their responsibility to perform rigorous tests to ensure that all the features of the craft are operating properly and in sync. On the design level their task is to evaluate ways to make improvements by observed aircraft performance. The average salary for an aeronautical Engineer is $102,155 yearly.
  • Electronics engineer: responsible for designing, developing, and implementing electronics equipment. This could vary from simple kitchen appliances to GPS systems or complex electric motors. They research designs, test components, and strive to meet the specifications required for their current projects. Electronics engineers also have to make sure that any components they are producing are compatible with other designs. The average salary of an Electronics engineer is $103,576 yearly.

Notable Electrical Engineers

Thomas Edison

            Edison grew up in the American Midwest, primarily Port Huron Michigan. Although he was not born there, the place of his birth was instead Milan Ohio in 1847. He was very interested in technology from a young age and as a young man he worked as a telegraph operator, familiarizing himself with the technology. Impoverished and living in a man’s basement in Elizibeth New jersey, Thomas Edison continued worked on the multiplex telegraphic system. This device would be his first financial success, allowing for two users to send messages simultaneously. He then produced the phonograph, an audio recording device that earned him widespread recognition for its novelty and ingenuity. What he is most known for today was the creation of the lightbulb. While he was not the first to ever attempt such a concept, his was by far the most effective, and so his breakthrough allowed electric light to be shined across the world.
