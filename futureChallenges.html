Future Challenges

Electrical Engineering Jobs

            The future for electrical engineering jobs is looking very bright, from 2021 to 2031 there is predicted to be a 6% growth in the career. With people retiring, passing away, or new jobs opening up each year, there are predicted to be around 21,100 job openings. With the development of many new technologies such as electric cars, AI, smart grids, and more, electrical engineers are becoming more needed (UC Riverside).

Expanding World Population and Pollution

            The population is growing at a rate of 1.3% a year, to put in perspective that is 77 people a year because of the growth vital resources are depleting in certain areas water, food, land, and even air. This is especially prevalent in third-world countries, especially in the case of water and food. As a result of the depleted resources, deforestation has been on the rise for the production of these resources and energy.

            Air pollution has also been a prevalent issue, this can be caused by certain fuels for energy, factories, and inefficient fuel usage. The energy will be discussed in the next section, but electrical engineering has been working efficiently to improve this issue, as well as fuel usage in cars, electrical engineers work on electrical vehicles. Air pollution can have many effects, such as poor air quality and acid rain. Through the efforts of many, including electrical engineers, air pollution has been improving, but there still is a way to go. This will be discussed later on in the energy section, but electrical engineers are working on finding less harmful sources of energy. This will be one of the biggest challenges for these engineers in the future.

This is one of the ways that electrical engineers are treating water pollution.

            Energy is one of the most prevalent things involving the future of electrical engineering. The consumption of energy is predicted to increase by 53% from 2008 to 2035. With this, renewable energy has become more popular. The United States wishes to be less dependent on other countries for energy and in 2012 the international dependence on energy was down 20% by 2035 the U.S. is predicted to be fuel efficient. Electrical engineers are needed for this process. Electrical engineers are working with fuel efficiency and developing new types of renewable energy. According to the book, renewable energy and fuel are the fastest-growing energy sources. Not only this, but renewable energy is much cleaner for the environment., With this, electrical engineers will work to help develop these types of energies. Such as solar, wind, hydro, and more. Electrical engineers play crucial roles when it comes to designing these forms of energy. Such as wind turbines, with the electrical engineers having to figure out how to harness the energy from the wind turbine and turn it into electricity.

Transportation and Infrastructure

            Wasted fuel from traffic has cost $121 billion. Electrical engineers have been given the difficult challenge of creating more fuel-efficient cars and transportation. This will help lower the wasted fuel. The main challenge for engineers is keeping up with the growing infrastructures and populations in the city to make sure everything runs smoothly, this is for all types of engineers. There are hundreds of millions of vehicles on the roads, and electrical engineers work on the efficiency of these up-and-coming vehicles to maximize efficiency and minimize waste.

            In 2021 the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act was passed and gave 1.2 trillion dollars to help improve infrastructure. For the electrical engineers this includes fixing challenges like transportation, electrical generation, transportation, the internet, and many more.

Future engineers will have to continue to develop weapons and this becomes a large problem for them to overcome and create.

            NASA also has been developing aircraft and satellites to help predict environmental change. Electrical engineers directly help with the creation of these as well. With climate disasters being so disastrous, Electrical engineers will only help develop these in the future. This is especially relevant with the rise in global temperatures and the risk that global warming presents to not only nature, but humans as well.
