Florida Polytechnic University

Florida Polytechnic University is a public university located in Lakeland, FL. The school has a total of 1,531 undergraduate students. Tuition and fees average around $4,940, and room and board costs another $12,219 per year. After financial aid, the total cost of attendance averages out to around $8,895 a year. Florida Poly has an 18:1 student-to-faculty ratio and a 31% 4-year graduation rate. It is important to note, however, that Florida Poly primarily has engineering majors enrolled there. It is typical for engineering students to graduate in 5-6 years, even without failing any classes, just because of the higher course load. Florida Poly is a somewhat selective school in its admissions process, with a 47% acceptance rate. The middle 50 percent of accepted students have a 1230-1390 SAT, a 26-31 ACT, and a 3.5 GPA [2]. Florida Institute of Technology

Florida Institute of Technology is a private university located in Melbourne, FL. The school has a total of 3,374 undergraduate students. Tuition and fees average around $44,360, and room and board costs another $14,340 per year. After financial aid, the total cost of attendance averages out to around $31,172 a year. Florida Tech has a 14:1 student-to-faculty ratio and a 50% 4-year graduation rate. The most popular majors at this school are also engineering, so this is an impressive graduation rate. Florida Tech is a somewhat selective school in its admissions process, with a 50% acceptance rate. The middle 50 percent of accepted students have an 1120-1320 SAT, a 22-28 ACT, and a 3.5 GPA [1].
